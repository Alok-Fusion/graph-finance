# 📊 Graph-Finance: Comprehensive Project Documentation

## 1. Problem Statement
The modern global financial ecosystem is fundamentally interconnected, yet traditional quantitative models (like standard MLPs or linear regressions) often fail to capture the non-linear, cascading effects of economic crises. When a localized shock occurs—such as a sudden liquidity crisis, a supply-chain collapse, or a regional volatility spike—its true threat lies in contagion. Standard models evaluate companies in isolation, entirely missing how a localized default in the "Manufacturing" sector can trigger a rapid, hidden structural collapse in the "Banking" sector through shared correlations and financial cross-holdings. 

## 2. Solution Overview
**Graph-Finance** solves this by leveraging state-of-the-art **Graph Neural Networks (GNNs)** to model the financial market precisely as what it is: a vast, interconnected network. 
By representing companies as nodes and their financial relationships as mathematical edges, the model can accurately simulate dual-shock scenarios (both positive and negative events), dynamically map how risk propagates across entire industrial sectors, and fundamentally classify companies based on their true systemic resilience (e.g., *Defensive vs. Risk-insulated*).

## 4. Project Overview & Objective
**Graph-Finance** is a state-of-the-art financial modeling system that leverages Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) to simulate and analyze systemic risk, economic shocks, and market contagion across interconnected companies and industrial sectors. 

By representing the financial ecosystem as a mathematical graph—where nodes are individual companies and edges represent their financial, cross-holding, or correlative relationships—the system captures complex, non-linear cascading effects. It answers the fundamental quantitative question: *If Company A experiences a massive liquidity shock today, how will that risk propagate to Sector B tomorrow?*

---

## 2. Model Architecture (`FinancialGNN`)
The core intelligence of Graph-Finance is driven by a bespoke, bounded PyTorch Geometric Graph Neural Network defined in `model/gnn_model.py`.

### 2.1 Layer Structure
The architecture is purposefully designed to capture both immediate local impacts and wider systemic spillovers.
- **Input Node Features:** Each node is assigned multiple features. The two most critical are:
  - `Index 1` (Volatility Index): The primary feature manipulated during shock scenarios.
  - `Index 3` (Volatility Sensitivity): An inherent multiplier dictating how susceptible a company is to localized volatility.
- **Layer 1 (`GCNConv` + ReLU + Dropout):** The first Graph Convolutional Network (GCN) layer ingests node features and the graph topology (`edge_index`). It aggregates neighbor data, applies a ReLU non-linearity, and uses Dropout (p=0.3) to prevent overfitting during unsupervised training.
- **Layer 2 (`GCNConv` + Residual Connection):** The second GCN layer captures 2nd-degree connections (e.g., how the shock moves from A to B, and then B to C). A residual connection (`h = h + h2`) ensures original node features are preserved, preventing the "oversmoothing" commonly seen in deep GNNs.
- **Output Layer:** A final linear transformation mapping the hidden states to a 1D risk score. This output is passed through a bounded non-linearity (`torch.tanh(out) * 5`) to preserve the asymmetrical nature of financial scenarios while preventing numerical explosion.

## 3. Training Methodology & Philosophy
Unlike typical supervised models, predicting financial crises lacks perfectly labeled, vast datasets. Graph-Finance solves this via a novel **Bounded Unsupervised Training approach**.

### 3.1 Unsupervised Loss Function (`train.py`)
The model optimizes using a custom, multi-part loss function that relies purely on network topology and mathematical constraints:
1. **Variance Constraint:** `(out.var() - TARGET_VAR) ** 2`
   - Forces the model's output distribution to maintain a set standard deviation, preventing trivial solutions where every company is assigned the exact same risk score.
2. **Graph Smoothness (Homophily):** `((out[src] - out[dst]) ** 2).mean()`
   - Penalizes drastic differences in the risk scores of highly connected nodes. It mathematically enforces the economic reality that intertwined entities share similar systemic destinies.
3. **Scale Regularization:** `(out ** 2).mean()`
   - Standard L2 regularization on the output magnitude, ensuring the predicted risk scores remain grounded near zero unless a severe shock is applied.

*Training Details:* Optimized via Adam (LR=0.005) over 300 epochs.

### 3.2 Baseline Implementations
To scientifically prove the utility of the GNN, the project includes two ablations:
- **`train_baseline.py`:** An MLP that ignores the graph structure entirely.
- **`train_no_corr.py`:** A GNN trained on a graph missing its correlative edges. 
Comparing these against the primary model confirms that message-passing over financial connections is the true source of predictive power.

## 4. Systemic Contagion & Simulation Scripts
Graph-Finance is built heavily around simulation. Beyond the interactive dashboard, it includes rigorous deterministic simulation scripts.

### 4.1 Single-Step Propagation (`simulate_propagation.py`)
A fast utility for injecting a raw volatility shock (e.g., `Magnitude = 3.0`) into a singular company and calculating the immediate mathematical $\Delta \text{Risk}$ across all other nodes in the network compared to their baseline state.

### 4.2 Multi-Step Temporal Evolution (`multi_step_simulation.py`)
Simulates how a crisis evolves across explicit timeframes ($T_0 ... T_n$).
- It allows for **simultaneous multi-node shocks**.
- Implements a **Decay Factor (`decay = 0.6`)**, modeling how initial market panic subsides or is absorbed by central entities over time.
- The temporal risk matrix is exported as a NumPy array (`risk_evolution.npy`) for external longitudinal analysis plotting.

## 5. Validation & Accuracy Experiments
Because the primary model is unsupervised, accuracy is evaluated via robustness and adherence to financial intuition, handled in `validation_random_shock.py`.

### 5.1 Random Shock Experiment (Monte Carlo Approach)
- **Methodology:** The script randomly selects nodes, applies heavy volatility shocks, and computes the network-wide $\Delta \text{Risk}$. This is repeated over 50 iterations (`N_RUNS = 50`).
- **Metric:** Records the **Average Impact (%)** and **Standard Deviation (%)** grouped by Industrial Sector. 
- **Purpose:** Proves that shocks propagate smoothly and logically, verifying the stability of the bounded $\tanh$ output layers.

### 5.2 Ablation Study
A subset of the validation suite tests the model's reliance on intrinsic company features:
- **Mechanism:** It removes the `Volatility Sensitivity` scaling factor during the shock injection.
- **Result:** Comparing this to the "Full Model" illustrates that the GNN successfully differentiates between "Risk-insulated" and "Leverage-sensitive" entities, rather than treating the market as a uniform, undifferentiated web.

## 6. Dual-Shock Scenario Dashboard
The core analytical platform is the interactive Streamlit user-interface (`shock_analysis_dashboard.py`).

### 6.1 Scenario Generation & Contagion Logic
Users can inject localized events (Positive Shocks / Negative Shocks). The UI explicitly models the immediate sector contagion before passing it strictly through the GNN:
1. **Primary Node:** Receives $S \times \text{Sensitivity}$.
2. **Direct Peers (Same Sector):** Suffer an immediate spillover penalty (`0.3 * S * Peer_Sensitivity`) due to shared industrial risk.
3. **GNN Output:** The model predicts the final non-linear $\Delta \text{Risk}$ map resulting from these inputs.

### 6.2 Economic Categorization Engine
The dashboard digests complex multi-dimensional outputs into four actionable classifications by comparing the dual-shock Net Effect against intrinsic Volatility Sensitivity:

**Company-Level Classifications:**
- **Defensive / Leverage-sensitive:** High intrinsic sensitivity, high positive net effect. (Dangerous in a crisis).
- **Volatility-benefiting (Convex payoff):** High intrinsic sensitivity, highly negative net effect. (Thrives in a crisis).
- **Risk-insulated / Stable:** Extremely low net effect. (Safe-haven assets).
- **Moderate asymmetric exposure:** The baseline expectation for a standard firm.

**Sector-Level Classifications:**
Aggregates company data to classify entire industries as either a *Defensive Sector* (overall net positive), a *Pro-cyclical Sector* (overall net negative), or *Neutral*.
